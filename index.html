I am a first-year Ph.D student in Machine Learning at the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial & Systems Engineering (ISyE), Georgia Tech. I'm fortunate to have received the Ronald J. and Carol T. Beerman Fellowship, and the ISyE Beerman President's Fellowship.

Previously, I studied at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, and graduated with a Bachelor of Statistics (B.Stat) degree (with Honours) in 2022, and a Master of Statistics (M.Stat) degree (with Theoretical Statistics specialization) in 2024.

My research interest lies in the intersection of statistics, machine learning, and optimization. I am interested in theory, methodologies, and their applications that make sense. Prior to joining Georgia Tech as a Ph.D student, I have mostly worked on high-dimensional classification (with Prof. Subhajit Dutta), clustering (with Prof. Swagatam Das), and conditional independence testing (with Prof. Eugene Katsevich). Apart from these, I spent the summer of 2022 at IIM Bangalore as a research intern.

I was born and raised in Kolkata, WB, India. I do not have a lot of hobbies, but have a firm interest in science, technology, cooking, football (soccer), table tennis, politics, and learning new languages (both human and programming languages).
